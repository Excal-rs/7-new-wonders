Petra is one of the oldest cities in the world, potentially founded in 312 BCE, and is the lost capital of the Nabataean kingdom, built into the mountain of Jabal Al-Madbah in modern Jordan. Its population consisted of nomadic Arabs who settled in the region for its proximity with incense trade routes, and the terrain of the city made it rather easy to defend from enemy attacks, which allowed for Petra to become an established wealthy city.

The lost city was only rediscovered in 1812 by a Swiss traveler. It consists of advanced water control structures, hidden access routes, impressive hellenistic architecture, an imponent city centre with a theater and a pool and garden complex, a high place of sacrifice, royal tombs and an exterior platform with a Monastery complex. A structure with special historical relevance is the Palace Tomb, speculated to have been the resting place of the kings and queens of Petra, as well as other prominent figures in the nabatean community.
